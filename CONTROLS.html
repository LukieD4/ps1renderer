PS1 Renderer — Control Sheet

Digital controller · latched modes · nothing held. Current mode shows top-left on the debug overlay.

All movement is delta-timed — held-button rates are per 1/60s and stay constant whether the scene runs at 60, 30 or 20fps, on NTSC or PAL. VB/DT on overlay page 1 show the frame's vblank count and the delta everything scaled by.

Held modifiers suspend the unmodified bindings they overlap: L2 pauses model rotation and FOV, L1+L2 additionally pauses roll. Near/far clamp themselves to what the current FOV can render, so the sliders simply stop rather than producing a broken ramp. Fog reads out on overlay page 1 (FOG / FOGDQA / LB); a ! after FOGDQA means the ramp railed, and LB shows which OT buckets the fog quads landed in. Layers 0 leaves only the GTE depth cue running — the direct A/B for what the quads contribute on textured geometry. The cull drops individual triangles past FAR, so distant models dissolve from their far side rather than vanishing whole. FOGCULL TRI/OBJ shows what it saved; RESID is how much of a textured surface still shows through at the cull plane — if you can see popping, raise LAYERS until RESID drops.

Six overlay pages, named in the header so a photographed frame identifies itself: RENDER (frame time, stage, culling, prims) · WORLD (camera, player, transforms) · LIGHT · FOG · AUDIO · AUDIOHW (CD pipeline + raw SPU registers). In DEBUG mode a bare R2 also flips the lighting path — the page count is even so a full lap returns it to where it started.
